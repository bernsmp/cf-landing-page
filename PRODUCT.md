# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Cognitive Fingerprint is for experts, coaches, consultants, operators, and creators with strong private judgment that is hard to explain from the outside. They arrive with messy source material: calls, notes, client sessions, voice memos, decisions, and repeated instincts they have not yet named.

## Product Purpose

The homepage should help visitors recognize that their expertise has structure, and that Cognitive Fingerprint can extract that structure from real source material so it becomes visible, usable, and easier to communicate.

## Brand Personality

Curious, precise, tactile. The brand should feel like an editorial field table for serious thought, not an AI product console.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-console surfaces, ornamental glow, pill-heavy badges, generic icon rows, neon/cyan/purple tech language, soft SaaS dashboards, and abstract neural-fingerprint metaphors that make the work feel generic.

## Design Principles

- Make the work feel physical through source cards, transcript scraps, margin notes, evidence objects, and paper-like layers.
- Let black space do real work, with CF gold used rarely and deliberately.
- Prefer concrete evidence over abstract feature language.
- Keep edges sharp, surfaces tactile, and motion restrained.
- Preserve the existing homepage copy unless Max explicitly asks for a messaging pass.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, readable mobile type, touch targets of at least 44px, and reduced-motion alternatives for decorative animation.
