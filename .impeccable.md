## Design Context

### Users
Cognitive Fingerprint is for experts, coaches, consultants, operators, and creators who have strong private judgment but struggle to show the structure behind it. They arrive with messy source material: calls, notes, client sessions, voice memos, and decisions. The homepage needs to help them recognize that their expertise can be extracted and made visible.

### Brand Personality
Curious, precise, tactile. The voice is conversational and observant, with real enthusiasm for the moment a hidden pattern becomes visible. The interface should feel like an editorial field table for serious thought, not an AI product console.

### Aesthetic Direction
Sharp cinematic black, CF gold restraint, layered physical artifacts, source cards, transcript scraps, margin notes, and evidence objects. Use `/public/logo/cf-t1.png` as the canonical logo mark. Avoid AI-console surfaces, glowing top haze, pill-heavy badges, generic icon rows, neon/cyan/purple tech language, and soft SaaS dashboards.

### Design Principles
- Make the work feel physical: cards, paper stacks, frames, notes, and visible source material.
- Let black space do work; use gold rarely and deliberately.
- Prefer concrete evidence over abstract feature language.
- Keep edges sharp and surfaces tactile, with restrained motion and no ornamental glow.
- Preserve Max Bernstein voice rules for all external-facing copy: concrete, curious, direct, no em dashes.
