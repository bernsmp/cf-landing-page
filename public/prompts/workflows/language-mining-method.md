### **â›ï¸ Phase 2: Raw Language Mining**

*"What are the exact words real people use?"*

**[AI-Executed]**

**The Key Principle:** We want RAW languageâ€”not summaries, not articles about the problem, but actual humans expressing their actual pain in their actual words.

**PROMPT:**

```

   - Frustration expressions ("I'm so tired of...")

   - How do they visualize the problem?
   - What comparisons do they make?
   - What imagery do they use?

   - Exact questions posted in forums
   - Questions in book reviews
   - Questions that reveal their mental model

5. FAILED SOLUTIONS MENTIONED
   - What have they tried?
   - Why did it fail?
   - How do they describe the failure?

6. DESIRED OUTCOMES
   - How do they describe "solved"?
   - What would success look like to them?
   - What metrics/timeframes do they mention?

7. OBJECTIONS AND SKEPTICISM
   - What are they skeptical about?
   - What objections do they raise to solutions?
   - What makes them hesitate?

FORMAT EACH FINDING AS:

---
**Quote:** "[Exact verbatim quote]"
**Source:** [Where found - be specific]
**Context:** [What prompted this - thread topic, book being reviewed, etc.]
**Category:** [Pain/Emotion/Metaphor/Question/Failed Solution/Outcome/Objection]
**Usability:** [How/where this could be used in messaging]
---

Prioritize:
- Quotes with strong emotional charge
- Metaphors that are vivid and specific
- Questions that reveal misunderstandings
- Failures that your solution actually addresses

Minimum extraction: 30 distinct quotes across categories
```

**QUALITY CHECK:**

The AI should return quotes that feel:
- **Raw** (not polished marketing language)
- **Specific** (not generic platitudes)
- **Emotional** (you can feel the frustration/fear/hope)
- **Attributable** (you know exactly where it came from)

If the quotes feel generic, push back: "These feel too polished. Find rawer, more emotional expressions."

## **Maintenance & Updates**

Each time you run this:

3. Add new findings, don't replace
